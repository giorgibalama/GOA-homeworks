Kingdom of Kartli

Introduction

The Kingdom of Kartli was a historical Georgian kingdom that emerged in the 4th century BC and lasted until the 5th century AD. It is also known as Iberia in classical sources. The kingdom played a significant role in the cultural and political development of Georgia and the Caucasus region.

History

Kartli, located in the eastern part of present-day Georgia, was one of the principal regions of ancient Georgia. The kingdom was established by the ancient Georgian tribes and later came under the influence of the Persian Empire, the Roman Empire, and the Byzantine Empire.

Culture

The Kingdom of Kartli was known for its rich culture, which included unique traditions, language, and art. Christianity was adopted as the state religion in the early 4th century, which significantly influenced the culture and traditions of the kingdom.

Notable Rulers

Some of the notable rulers of the Kingdom of Kartli include:

  • Pharnavaz I of Iberia (c. 302 – c. 237 BC) - the founder of the kingdom
  • Mirian III (c. 284 – c. 361 AD) - the king who adopted Christianity
  • Vakhtang I Gorgasali (c. 447 – c. 522 AD) - known for his military campaigns and church-building activities

Legacy

The Kingdom of Kartli laid the foundation for the later unified Georgian state. Its legacy can be seen in the cultural and historical heritage of modern Georgia. The region's historical sites and monuments attract many visitors and researchers interested in the ancient history of the Caucasus.
