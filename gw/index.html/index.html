Sataflia is located in Imereti, in the territory of Gumbra, Banoja and Khomuli villages of Tskaltubo municipality, on Sataflia mountain, 500 meters above sea level. It was established in 1935. The monument is complex in nature and contains geological, paleontological, speleological, zoological and botanical monuments.
Sataflia is part of the protected area of ​​Imereti and its infrastructure includes: a visitor center, a dinosaur footprint conservation building, an exhibition hall, a glass panoramic view, cafes, souvenir shops, a well-equipped cave and marked trails.
Visitors can visit fossilized footprints of dinosaurs, Sataflia karst cave, which is 900 meters long, 10 meters high and 12 meters wide, rock trail, Colchi forest, panoramic views.
Sataflia means "place of honey". The name originates from the Honeymoon Mountain, which got its name from the ancient tradition of collecting honey from the bees living in the mountain.
